# Project 4: AI Incident Response & Regulatory Escalation Scenario

## Scenario

**Organisation:** NorthStar Financial Services
**Incident:** CreditScore Pro (NS-AI-001) is found to produce systematically lower credit scores for applicants from specific postcodes that correlate with protected ethnic and socioeconomic characteristics.

The incident is detected in March 2026, two months after NorthStar's AI governance programme was established. It demonstrates that governance matters even for systems that were already in production — and that how you respond to an AI failure is as important as preventing it.

---

## What This Project Demonstrates

- Understanding of AI governance as **an ongoing discipline**, not a point-in-time compliance exercise
- Knowledge of how to structure an **AI incident response** — detection, triage, investigation, remediation, review
- Awareness of **regulatory escalation obligations** under the EU AI Act and financial services sector regulation
- Practical understanding of the NIST AI RMF **Manage** function
- Ability to apply **root cause analysis** methodology to AI systems

## Why This Project Matters

AI incidents are not hypothetical. Discriminatory credit scoring, biased recruitment tools, and opaque automated decisions have already produced regulatory action, litigation, and significant reputational damage at real organisations.

The EU AI Act does not just regulate AI at deployment. It establishes obligations for the entire operational lifecycle, including:

- Article 9: Ongoing risk management
- Article 12: Logging and traceability
- Article 17: Post-market monitoring
- Article 73: Reporting of serious incidents to national authorities

This project simulates what a well-prepared organisation does when an AI system causes harm. The quality of the response — how fast the issue is contained, how thoroughly it is investigated, how honestly it is reported — determines whether a bad situation becomes a manageable problem or a regulatory crisis.

## Frameworks Applied

- **NIST AI RMF** — Manage function (incident response, recovery, feedback)
- **EU AI Act** — Articles 9, 12, 17, 73 (ongoing monitoring, logging, post-market monitoring, incident reporting)
- **ISO/IEC 27035** — Incident management principles (adapted for AI context)
- **FCA Principles for Businesses** — Principle 6 (customers' interests), Principle 11 (relations with regulators)
